# 03. Final Week Checklist

## What to Finish First

If time is limited, finish these parts first:

1. A clear problem statement.
2. A clear dataset and target.
3. At least one useful visualization.
4. At least one trained and evaluated model.
5. A short explanation of the result.
6. A short note on limitations and ethics.

## What a Strong Submission Makes Easy to See

A strong submission makes these parts easy to find:

- What is the problem.
- What data was used.
- How the data was prepared.
- What model or models were used.
- How the result was evaluated.
- What limitations and ethical concerns matter.

If these points are easy to see, the work becomes easier to assess fairly.

## Final Practical Checklist

Before submission, check:

- My folder structure is understandable.
- My notebook or script is readable.
- My visualizations are saved or clearly visible.
- My evaluation is honest and connected to the task.
- My report or PDF explains the workflow clearly.
- My limitations section says something meaningful.
- My ethics note connects to the actual dataset or use case.
- My submission is complete enough to review without guesswork.

## If You Still Have Time After the Core Is Done

Only after the core is clear, you may want to:

- Clean up naming and structure.
- Improve one visualization.
- Compare one more model.
- Add one small improvement that you can still explain clearly.

These are extras, not the foundation.

## Final Reminder

At this stage, clarity is usually more valuable than ambition. A smaller project that is complete, understandable, and
honest is a great way to finish the course.
