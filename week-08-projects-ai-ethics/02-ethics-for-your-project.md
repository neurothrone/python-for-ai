# 02. Ethics for Your Project

## Why This Matters

Ethics is not something separate from the technical workflow. It is part of doing a project responsibly.

In a beginner project, ethics often means noticing where the work could be unfair, misunderstood, overtrusted, or
used in the wrong way.

## Four Practical Areas to Reflect On

### 1. Bias and Fairness

Ask:

- Does the dataset represent different groups fairly?
- Could the model work better for some groups than for others?
- Could earlier human decisions have shaped the data biasedly?

### 2. Privacy and Sensitive Data

Ask:

- Does the dataset contain personal, health, financial, or school-related data?
- Should some columns be treated more carefully?
- Would it be risky to share too much detail about individuals?

### 3. Misuse and Overtrust

Ask:

- Could anyone trust the model result too much and use it as a final decision?
- Could a simple model be used in a situation where it is not reliable enough?
- Could a high score make people trust the system too much?

### 4. Human Responsibility and Limitations

Ask:

- Where should a human still make the final judgment?
- What can the model not see or understand?
- What important limitation should be stated clearly?

## A Simple Ethics Reflection Structure

A short and useful ethics note often includes:

1. One realistic risk in the data or workflow.
2. One way the result could be misunderstood or misused.
3. One sentence about careful or responsible use.

That is often enough to show thoughtful reflection.

## Example Reflection Starters

You can adapt sentence starters like these to your own project:

- One possible bias in my dataset is...
- One group that may be underrepresented is...
- One reason my result should be interpreted carefully is...
- This model should not use itself for...
- A human should still review...

## Ethics Does Not Mean You Need a Perfect Answer

You do not need to solve every ethical issue. You need to show that you can:

- Notice realistic concerns.
- Connect them to your own project.
- Write about them clearly and honestly.

## Keep It Grounded in Your Own Work

The strongest ethics reflection is usually not very long. It is specific. It connects directly to:

- Your dataset.
- Your target.
- Your use case.
- Your limitations.

That is more useful than very general statements about AI.
