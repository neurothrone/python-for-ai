# 03. AI Ethics and Human Bias

## Why This Matters

AI systems can affect real people. Even a simple system can become unfair if the data, rules, or goals behind it are
poorly chosen.

## Ethics in Simple Words

In this course, ethics means thinking about whether an AI system is:

- Fair.
- Safe.
- Responsible.
- Suitable for the real situation where it is used.

Ethics is not only about advanced AI. It also matters in beginner projects because the habits start early.

## Human Bias Can Enter AI Systems

One important risk is **human bias** (an unfair pattern in how people think or decide). If old human decisions were
unfair, an AI system trained on that old data can learn the same unfair pattern.

Example:

- If a hiring dataset reflects unfair choices from the past.
- A model trained on that dataset may repeat or strengthen that unfairness.

The model is not magically neutral just because it uses code and data.

## Simple Questions to Ask

Before trusting an AI system, ask:

- Who could be helped by this system?
- Who could be harmed by this system?
- Does the data represent the people the system will affect?
- Could the system repeat an unfair pattern from old human decisions?
- Should a human review the result before an important decision is made?

## Good Habits

- Check where the data came from.
- Be careful with strong claims about what the model can do.
- Keep human review for sensitive decisions.
- Explain simple limits and risks when presenting results.

## Key Idea to Keep

AI can be useful, but useful is different from fair. Human bias in the data can become bias in the system.
