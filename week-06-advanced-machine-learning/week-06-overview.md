# Week 6 Overview: Advanced Machine Learning

This page is the starting point for Week 6.

## Week Goal

By the end of Week 6, students should be able to explain the basic idea of classification, train a
`KNeighborsClassifier` and a `DecisionTreeClassifier`, read simple evaluation results, and compare two models on the
same classification problem.

## Week 6 and the Project

Week 6 is the bridge between your first machine learning workflow in Week 5 and the final individual project later in
the course.

This week helps you practice a clearer workflow:

- Choose one clear question.
- Inspect the data before modeling.
- Use a simple model first.
- Evaluate the result honestly.
- Compare models fairly before trusting one result too quickly.

> [!NOTE]
> - If your final project is a classification problem, this week gives you two good first models to start from.
> - If your final project is a regression problem instead, the exact models will change, but the workflow habits from
    this week still apply.

You do not need to understand every new word at once. The goal this week is to take the workflow step by step and build
confidence as you go.

## Week 6 Completion Checklist

- I can explain the difference between regression and classification in clear terms.
- I can train and use a `KNeighborsClassifier`.
- I can train and use a `DecisionTreeClassifier`.
- I can explain why `k-NN` cares about feature value ranges.
- I can read accuracy, a confusion matrix, and a simple classification report.
- I can explain what cross-validation does in a simple way.
- I can explain data leakage in clear words and why it makes evaluation less trustworthy.
- I can compare two classification models on the same dataset without mixing up the workflow.
